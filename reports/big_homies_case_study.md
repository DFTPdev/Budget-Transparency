## RECOMMENDATIONS

### For Immediate Implementation:

1. **Require Sub-Recipient Reporting**
   - NGOs receiving >$250K must report all sub-recipients
   - Include amounts, purposes, and outcomes
   - Publish in searchable database

2. **Flag Non-Competitive Awards**
   - CARDINAL should label formula allocations vs. competitive grants
   - Require justification for non-competitive awards >$100K

3. **Link Payments to Legislative Actions**
   - Every grant should reference authorizing legislation or amendment
   - Include legislator names and vote records

4. **Publish Grant Selection Criteria**
   - DCJS must publish RFP documents
   - Scoring rubrics and selection committee members
   - Winning proposals (redacted if necessary)

5. **Outcome Reporting**
   - Quarterly reports on program metrics
   - Cost-per-participant analysis
   - Independent evaluation of effectiveness

### For Long-Term Reform:

6. **Eliminate "Black Box" Programs**
   - Break down mega pass-through programs like "Fin Asst-Admin of Justice Scvs"
   - Create specific line items for major recipients

7. **Competitive Grant Requirement**
   - All NGO grants >$100K must be competitively awarded
   - Exception process requires legislative approval

8. **Pass-Through Entity Registry**
   - Maintain public database of organizations that redistribute state funds
   - Require annual disclosure of sub-recipient networks

## CONCLUSION

Big Homies Inc. represents a **textbook case** of how state funding can be obscured through:
- Non-competitive grant processes
- Pass-through entities
- Lack of legislative transparency
- Inadequate outcome reporting
- Single-program concentration

While the organization may be providing valuable youth services, the **funding mechanism raises serious transparency and accountability concerns**. Taxpayers have no visibility into:
- How recipients are selected
- Who ultimately receives the funds
- What outcomes are achieved
- Whether competitive alternatives were considered

**This case study demonstrates the urgent need for comprehensive grant transparency reforms in Virginia's budget process.**
